Thank-You Email -- After Your Meeting

Send within 24 hours of the in-person meeting. The yellow placeholders are what you fill in.

How to use this

  1. Right after your meeting, jot down the one specific thing they cared about most. Anything: a schedule date, a pain point with a past sub, a budget number, a scope concern, even something personal.
  2. Open your email, paste the template below, and replace each yellow placeholder with the real detail.
  3. Send within 24 hours of the meeting. Faster is better. Most subs never send anything.
  4. One sentence about something personal at the end is fine if it fit the tone of the meeting. Skip it if the meeting was strictly business.

The template

To: [GC primary contact email]

One thing I want to commit to in writing before you make a decision: [the specific commitment you heard them care about most -- examples: "I will be on site personally every day during rough-in," or "I will have the submittal package on your desk within three weeks of award," or "I will coordinate with the other trades before any work gets hung in the ceiling"]. That is the one I will not move on.

Why this email works

  • It is fast. Within 24 hours of the meeting puts you in the top 5% of subs who follow up at all.
  • It is specific. Generic "thanks for your time" emails are forgettable. Naming the specific thing they care about and the specific commitment you are making puts you in their long-term memory.
  • It quotes the bid number. Reinforces it without being pushy.
  • It addresses their concerns directly. Shows you were listening, not waiting to talk.
  • It ends with a personal note. Optional, but it differentiates you from out-of-area bidders who treat every project like a transaction.
